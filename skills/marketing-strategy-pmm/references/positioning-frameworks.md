# Positioning Frameworks

## April Dunford's 5-Step Positioning Process

### Step 1: Identify Competitive Alternatives
Understand what customers use instead of your product:
- **Direct competitors**: Same category, same solution
- **Adjacent competitors**: Different category, solve same problem
- **Status quo**: What customers do today manually

### Step 2: Determine Unique Attributes
Identify features/capabilities only you have:
- Product features
- Technology approach
- Business model
- Customer service
- Brand positioning

### Step 3: Map Attributes to Business Value
Connect unique attributes to customer outcomes:
- Why does this attribute matter?
- What business problem does it solve?
- What is the quantified value?

### Step 4: Define Best-Fit Customers
Determine who cares most about your unique value:
- Which customer segment gets the most value?
- Who has the most acute problem?
- Who can afford your solution?

### Step 5: Choose Market Category Strategy
Select your competitive positioning:
- **Head-to-head**: Compete directly in existing category
- **Niche domination**: Own a specific segment
- **Category creation**: Define a new market space

## Geoffrey Moore Positioning Statement Template

```
FOR [target customer]
WHO [statement of need/pain]
THE [product name] IS A [category]
THAT [key benefit]
UNLIKE [competitive alternative]
OUR PRODUCT [primary differentiation]

[Product] helps [target customer] achieve [business goal]
by [unique approach], resulting in [quantified outcome].
```

## Positioning Validation Interview Protocol

### Interview Questions

1. **Problem Discovery**
   - "What problem does [Product] solve for you?"
   - "How did you solve this before?"

2. **Value Validation**
   - "What specific outcome have you achieved?"
   - "How would you describe [Product] to a colleague?"

3. **Differentiation**
   - "What makes [Product] different from alternatives?"
   - "Why did you choose [Product] over others?"

4. **Language Test**
   - Use their exact words in your positioning
   - Avoid jargon they don't use

### Validation Criteria

- 7+ customers describe value using same language
- Unprompted value statements in reviews
- Sales team uses positioning consistently
- Marketing materials reflect validated language

## Competitive Positioning Map Construction

### 2x2 Matrix Framework

|                 | Low Price                 | High Price               |
|-----------------|--------------------------|--------------------------|
| **High Feature**   | Value Leader             | Premium Solution         |
| **Low Feature**    | Economy Option           | Legacy/Overpriced        |

### Strategic Axis Selection

Choose axes based on:
1. What differentiation matters most to buyers?
2. Where are competitors positioned?
3. Where is whitespace in the market?
